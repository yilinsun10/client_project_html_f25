Let's Begin Our Internship Search!

Plan Your Search and Move with Confidence

A focused internship search saves time and leads to better results. Use this page to help clarify your goals, organize your materials, and run a simple weekly routine that keeps you on job-searching track.

UMSI students succeed when they combine a strong resume and portfolio with targeted applications and consistent networking. The resources below are curated with the UMSI Career Development Office (CDO) in mind.

Need guidance? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach to customize your plan.

Weekly Search Routine (5 Steps)

  1. Define your targets: Roles (e.g., UX Research, Data Analyst), industries, and locations.
  2. Refresh materials: Tailor your resume and portfolio to the week’s top roles.
  3. Source roles: Check Career Link, Handshake, company sites, and curated lists.
  4. Apply with intent: Quality over quantity—address requirements and keywords.
  5. Follow up & network: Send at least 2–3 outreach notes and track all statuses.

Targeting & Tailoring Checklist

  • Highlight the top 2–3 skills each posting emphasizes (e.g., SQL, Figma, stakeholder interviews).
  • Mirror critical keywords in your bullets (truthfully) to pass initial screens.
  • Include one short, relevant project or result near the top of your experience section.
  • If a portfolio is requested, link directly to the most relevant case study page.

Internship Search FAQ

  • When should I start?
    Many summer internships post in early fall and continue through spring. Begin prep now and check portals weekly.
  • How many applications per week?
    Aim for consistent, high-quality applications (e.g., 5–10/week) plus 2–3 targeted networking messages.
  • Is unpaid work okay?
    Prioritize paid opportunities. If considering unpaid roles, discuss with CDO to weigh learning value and finances.
  • International student considerations?
    Confirm visa-related requirements (e.g., CPT,OPT) with the International Center and your program; start early.
  • How do I stand out?
    Demonstrate impact with quantified results, tailor to the role, and show relevant projects/portfolios up front.
