Networking & Connections

Make Connections that Move You Forward

Networking is about building genuine relationships that can support your career growth. Whether you’re exploring fields, seeking advice, or looking for opportunities, connecting with others can open doors and provide valuable insights.

Not sure where to begin? Pick one role you’re curious about, find 3–5 alumni or professionals in that area, send two short messages, and schedule one 15-minute chat this week.

Need feedback on your approach? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach.

Networking Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Choose a focus: roles or teams you want to learn about.
  2. Find people: alumni, speakers, or professionals connected to your focus.
  3. Send short outreach: under ~100 words with a clear, small ask (15 minutes).
  4. Have the conversation: prepare 3–4 questions; share a brief story about your interests.
  5. Follow up: send a thank-you within 24–48 hours and note next steps.

Networking FAQ

  • What do I say in the first message?
    Keep it brief: who you are, why you’re reaching out, and a small ask (15 minutes, 2 quick questions).
  • What if they don’t respond?
    Follow up once a week later. If no reply, move on and try the next person.
  • How do I prepare for a chat?
    Read their profile, skim a project or talk, and bring 3–4 questions (TIARA is a good guide).
  • How do I maintain the connection?
    Send a short thank-you, connect on LinkedIn, and share an update in 4–8 weeks.
